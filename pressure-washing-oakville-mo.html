Walkways lose their original color. The home still looks familiar because you see it every day, but compared to photos from five or ten years ago, the difference can be dramatic.

Why Oakville homes get dirty

Oakville’s established character — mature neighborhoods, large shade trees, attractive landscaping — is also exactly what contributes to exterior staining.

Shade creates moisture

Areas shaded throughout the day stay damp longer after rain, dew, and humidity — ideal conditions for algae and mildew, especially on north-facing siding, fences, retaining walls, concrete driveways, sidewalks, and patios. It often starts as faint green discoloration before becoming darker and more widespread.

Tree coverage increases organic debris

Leaves, pollen, twigs, and organic matter continually settle onto exterior surfaces. As they break down, they contribute to staining. Driveways under heavy tree cover darken significantly faster than those in full sun.

Missouri weather does the rest

Spring pollen, summer humidity, fall leaves, and winter moisture all add to the gradual buildup. None of this means neglect — it’s simply a reality of homeownership in South County.

Driveway cleaning in Oakville

Driveway cleaning is often the first service homeowners consider because results are immediate and visible. Concrete is naturally porous, so it absorbs and retains dirt and contaminants over time. Many homeowners assume their driveway is permanently stained — in reality, much of what they’re seeing is surface buildup.

Common driveway problems: algae growth, dirt accumulation, leaf staining, tire marks, and general weathering. A properly cleaned driveway often appears significantly brighter and more uniform — homeowners frequently tell us they forgot what the concrete originally looked like.

Driveway cleaning makes sense when the surface looks darker than surrounding concrete, algae is visible, it hasn’t been cleaned in years, or you want better curb appeal before guests or an event. It may not make sense when severe spalling or structural deterioration is present — we’d rather give an honest recommendation than sell a service that won’t meet expectations.

House washing in Oakville

House washing is one of the most misunderstood exterior services. Many homeowners hear “pressure washing” and assume the whole home gets blasted with extreme pressure — in reality, most homes benefit from a much gentler approach.

The same environmental conditions that affect driveways affect siding: green algae, black streaking, mildew, spider webs, dirt, and pollen, especially on north-facing walls and shaded sections. Excessive pressure can damage siding, remove paint, force water behind exterior materials, and leave visible marks — which is why most residential projects rely on soft washing instead: specialized cleaning solutions and controlled application that target the source of staining rather than blasting the surface. It’s used on vinyl siding, brick, stucco, fiber cement, soffits, fascia, and trim — each material needs a different approach.

For many Oakville homeowners, house washing is one of the highest-impact exterior improvements available without a renovation — brighter, better maintained, more inviting, more visually appealing.

Pressure washing vs. soft washing

They’re not the same thing. Pressure washing uses pressurized water on durable surfaces — concrete, sidewalks, driveways, patios. Soft washing uses lower-pressure methods for more delicate materials like siding. Many homeowners search “pressure washing Oakville” when what they actually need is soft washing — understanding the difference ensures the right method gets used on the right surface.

When pressure washing isn’t worth the money

Most pressure washing companies won’t include this section. We think it matters — not every surface needs professional cleaning, and not every stain can be removed.

Severely damaged concrete

Cleaning removes dirt, algae, and surface contamination. It does not repair cracks, settling, spalling, or structural deterioration.

Faded materials

Cleaning can remove buildup. It cannot reverse years of UV exposure on faded siding or aging paint.

Rotten wood

Cleaning is not a substitute for repair. If a deck, fence, or trim board has begun deteriorating, address that before cosmetic cleaning.

Unrealistic expectations: pressure washing can create dramatic improvement. It cannot make a 25-year-old surface look brand new. If we believe expectations and likely results don’t align, we’ll tell you before any work begins.

How often should I pressure wash my driveway?

Most driveways benefit from cleaning every one to three years. Properties with heavy shade often need more frequent cleaning.

How often should siding be washed?

Most homes benefit from washing every one to three years depending on tree coverage and moisture conditions.

What causes green streaks on siding?

In many cases, algae growth is the culprit. North-facing walls and shaded surfaces are especially vulnerable.

Yes, when performed correctly. Improper techniques can damage concrete, which is why surface-specific methods matter.

Some oil stains can be improved significantly. Older stains that have deeply penetrated concrete may not disappear completely.

Do shaded homes need cleaning more often?
